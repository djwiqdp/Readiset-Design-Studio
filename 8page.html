Cheers to Your Eyes

188*257mm

An art book adaptation of Keigo Higashino's short story “Cheers to Your Eyes” employs an experimental design. The left side of the book is oriented upright, while the right side is inverted, allowing readers to physically rotate the book at the pivotal moment when the male protagonist’s illusion about the woman he is interested in shatters. In other words, the book is structured to emphasize the narrative twist: after reading through the left pages,readers turn the book around to navigate the right pages, eventually returning to the starting point. Black graphics represent reality, while pink-hued graphics symbolize fantasy. As the story progresses, the fantasy gradually encroaches on reality, depicting the collapse of the illusion. This is visually reinforced by the increasingly distorted text within the fantasy graphics.
